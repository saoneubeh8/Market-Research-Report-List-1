<p><p>The antidepressant drugs market encompasses various types, each with distinct mechanisms. Tricyclic antidepressants target multiple neurotransmitters, while serotonin-norepinephrine inhibitors focus on serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake. Atypical antipsychotics address mood disorders alongside psychotic symptoms. Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors specifically enhance serotonin levels. Benzodiazepines are primarily used for anxiety but can alleviate depressive symptoms. Tetracyclic antidepressants influence multiple neurotransmitters, and monoamine oxidase inhibitors inhibit the enzyme that breaks down neurotransmitters, enhancing mood. Additional categories encompass newer formulations and therapies for depression.</p></p>
