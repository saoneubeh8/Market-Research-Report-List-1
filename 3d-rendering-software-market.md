<p><p>The 3D rendering software market is segmented into two main types: on-premise software and on-demand software. On-premise software is installed locally on a user's hardware, providing enhanced control and security but requiring significant upfront investment and ongoing maintenance. In contrast, on-demand software, often cloud-based, offers flexibility and scalability, allowing users to access powerful rendering capabilities without extensive local resources. This model typically operates on a subscription basis, making it more accessible for smaller studios and individual artists.</p></p>
