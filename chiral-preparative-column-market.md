<p><p>The chiral preparative column market comprises various types, each tailored for specific separation processes. Cellulose-based columns utilize natural polysaccharides for enantiomer resolution. Cyclodextrin columns feature cyclic oligosaccharides, enhancing selectivity in separating chiral compounds. Macrolide antibiotics serve as chiral selectors for complex mixtures, while protein-based columns leverage affinity interactions for biopharmaceutical purification. Ligand exchange columns facilitate chiral resolution by exchanging ligands with target molecules. Prikle (practical-kle) columns utilize unique polymeric structures, while 'others' encompass emerging and niche technologies in chiral separation.</p></p>
