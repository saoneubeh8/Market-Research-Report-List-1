<p><p>The wet process phosphoric acid market consists of three primary production methods: the nitric acid method, hydrochloric acid method, and sulfuric acid method. The nitric acid method utilizes nitric acid to produce phosphoric acid from phosphate rock, typically yielding higher purity. The hydrochloric acid method employs hydrochloric acid for a similar purpose but often results in more impurities. The sulfuric acid method, the most widely used, reacts sulfuric acid with phosphate rock, creating phosphoric acid and gypsum, making it cost-effective and efficient.</p></p>
