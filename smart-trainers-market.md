<p><p>The Smart Trainers market is primarily categorized into two types: Wheel-On and Direct Drive. </p><p>Wheel-On trainers have the bicycle's rear wheel resting on a roller, making them easier to set up and generally more affordable. They provide a good balance of performance and convenience for casual cyclists. </p><p>Direct Drive trainers, on the other hand, require removing the rear wheel and attaching the bike directly to the trainer. They offer superior stability, accuracy, and responsiveness, appealing to serious cyclists and competitive athletes.</p></p>
